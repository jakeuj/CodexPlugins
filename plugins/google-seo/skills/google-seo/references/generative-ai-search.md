# Generative AI Search

Source: Google Search Central, "Optimizing your website for generative AI features on Google Search"
URL: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide
Source page last updated: 2026-06-05 UTC. Checked from Codex on 2026-06-10.

Use this reference when the user asks about AI Overviews, AI Mode, generative AI search visibility, "AEO", "GEO", or whether AI changes SEO priorities.

## Core Position

- Foundational SEO remains relevant for Google generative AI search.
- Google's generative AI features are rooted in core Search ranking and quality systems.
- These systems rely on content from Google's Search index; a page still needs crawlability, indexability, and useful content before AI visibility is realistic.
- Treat "AEO" and "GEO" advice cautiously. From Google Search's perspective, optimizing for generative AI search is still SEO.

## Content Priorities

- Create valuable, non-commodity content for a real audience.
- Add unique viewpoints, original experience, first-hand perspective, or expert-led analysis.
- Avoid simply restating content that already exists elsewhere or could be produced generically.
- Organize content with clear sections, helpful headings, and readable paragraphs.
- Add high-quality images and video when they genuinely support the page.

## Eligibility And Controls

- If a page blocks indexing, blocks snippets, or is not eligible to show in Search, do not expect it to appear in Google generative AI features.
- Before AI-specific work, confirm normal SEO basics: crawlability, indexability, canonical URL, helpful content, snippets, images, and structured data where appropriate.
- Snippet and preview controls can affect how content is used or displayed in Search features; audit robots meta tags, `data-nosnippet`, and supported meta tags when needed.

## What To Avoid

- Do not present "AEO" or "GEO" as a magic separate optimization track for Google.
- Do not recommend unnecessary AI text files, artificial "chunking" purely for AI, or inauthentic mentions as shortcuts.
- Do not replace original expertise with generic AI-written summaries.
- Do not over-optimize for imagined AI behavior at the expense of human usefulness.

## Agentic Experiences

- Browser agents and future protocols may inspect rendered pages, DOM structure, screenshots, and accessibility trees.
- Treat agent-friendliness as an extension of good web fundamentals: accessible markup, clear content, stable UI, and machine-readable structure where appropriate.
- Only prioritize agentic-specific changes after core Google Search SEO and user experience are healthy.
